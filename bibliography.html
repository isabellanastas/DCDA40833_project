Research Methodology

Key Sources & Research Approach

Overview

This project uses a mixed-methods approach to explore how visual aesthetics on social media shape what people perceive as healthy, especially in food and wellness content. I combined survey data with outside research to better understand how people respond to aesthetic versus non-aesthetic health content online. By looking at both audience responses and existing research, this methodology helps connect what people see on platforms like TikTok and Instagram to how they interpret health, credibility, and desirability.

Research Approach

This project is centered around one main question: how do visual aesthetics on social media influence what people perceive as healthy? To answer that, I used a mixed-methods approach that combines both qualitative and quantitative research.

I started with secondary research from my annotated bibliography to build a foundation around digital wellness culture, social media influence, and the way aesthetics shape perception online. These sources helped frame the bigger picture and gave context for how visual presentation can influence trust, engagement, and credibility.

From there, I paired that research with original survey data to better understand how people actually respond to this kind of content in practice. Combining those two methods helped me compare what existing research says with how people are actually interpreting health content online.

Data Collection

This project uses two main forms of data collection: secondary source research and original survey data.

The first phase focused on secondary sources gathered through my annotated bibliography. These included academic journal articles, media analysis, and digital culture research related to wellness trends, food media, self-presentation, and platform aesthetics. These sources helped build the foundation for the project and identify common themes in how health is presented online.

The second phase was an original survey distributed to participants within my target demographic of social media users. The survey asked respondents to evaluate food and wellness content based on how healthy, trustworthy, and visually appealing it seemed. Participants were shown examples of aesthetic and non-aesthetic posts and asked to compare how each shaped their perception. This helped me collect direct insight into how visual presentation influences the way people interpret health content.

Analytical Framework

This project is analyzed through a digital media and visual culture lens, with a focus on social media self-presentation, aesthetic labor, and health perception. More specifically, I looked at how visual choices like lighting, composition, color, styling, and overall polish influence the way people interpret wellness content online.

I compared aesthetic and non-aesthetic examples of food content to identify patterns in audience perception and engagement. Survey responses were analyzed for recurring trends in how people associated visual polish with health, credibility, and desirability. I then compared those findings back to the themes that came up in my secondary research to see how closely audience behavior aligned with what existing research already suggests.

Research Limitations

There are a few limitations to this project that are important to acknowledge. First, the survey sample size was relatively small, largely because I had limited reach when distributing it, so the responses reflect a narrower audience than I originally hoped for. Because the survey was shared within a peer-based network, many respondents likely had similar social media habits, demographics, and assumptions, which limits how broadly the results can be applied.

Second, perceptions of health are subjective and can be influenced by personal experience, dietary habits, and existing beliefs around wellness. That means responses were shaped not just by visual design, but also by individual bias.

Lastly, social media trends move quickly, and the examples used in this project reflect a specific moment in digital wellness culture. Because of that, these findings are most useful as a snapshot of current behavior rather than a fixed or universal conclusion.

AI Usage Statement

I also used AI as a support tool throughout this project, primarily to assist with the technical development of the website. GitHub's built-in AI tools were especially helpful in troubleshooting HTML and CSS, setting up page structure, and refining the overall visual design of the site. AI was used to support coding, layout, and styling decisions, but all research direction, analysis, written content, and final interpretations were developed by me.
