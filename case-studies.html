Social Media Case Studies

Trends, Accounts & Content Analysis

Overview

This section provides detailed analysis of specific TikTok and Instagram trends, accounts, and content that exemplify the research findings. Each case study includes screenshots, analysis, and discussion of how the content relates to the broader research questions.

Case Study 1: Aesthetic Yogurt Bowl

Post: Louise Seghers, "Heavenly Breakfast Combi" (TikTok, Nov. 8, 2024) | Platform: TikTok | Date Analyzed: April 21, 2026

Engagement Metrics: 6,475 followers | 247.7k views | 4,599 likes | 17 comments | 3,819 saves | 315 shares

Analysis

Context & Background

This post presents a chia yogurt breakfast bowl styled in a highly curated, visually polished way. The creator frames the meal as part of an idealized "good morning" routine, emphasizing softness, wellness, and beauty over function. It fits into the broader "clean girl wellness" trend on TikTok, where health is communicated through minimalist visuals, delicate styling, and aspirational routines.

Key Content Characteristics

This post relies heavily on aesthetic visual codes to communicate health. The bowl is carefully arranged with each topping placed in distinct sections, creating balance and symmetry. The neutral background, soft lighting, and overhead camera angle make the food appear clean and intentional. Ingredients like chia pudding, pomegranate seeds, banana, yogurt, and nut butter are arranged less like a meal and more like a styled composition.

The colors are muted and cohesive, with soft whites, beige tones, and subtle pops of red. This controlled palette reinforces the idea of health as visually calm, elevated, and aesthetically pleasing. The text overlay ("good morning 💗 let's make a chia yogurt breakfast bowl") adds softness and intimacy, framing the post as part of a lifestyle rather than just a recipe.

How It Supports My Research

This post supports my argument that social media often presents health as something aesthetic first and nutritional second. The visual appeal of the bowl is what drives attention, while the actual nutritional value is secondary to the way it is styled. Health is communicated through cleanliness, symmetry, and visual softness rather than explicit nutritional education.

Notable Aesthetic & Cultural Elements

This post reflects the broader wellness aesthetic commonly seen in TikTok food content: minimalist plating, soft natural lighting, neutral tones, and "effortless" beauty. It aligns with a feminine, aspirational version of health that is tied to self-care, routine, and visual discipline. The meal functions as both food and identity marker, signaling a specific lifestyle associated with wellness culture online.

Engagement Patterns

This post performs strongly, with high engagement across likes, saves, and shares. Its success likely comes from its visual polish and ability to fit into familiar aesthetic trends. The content is highly saveable because it is both visually aspirational and easy to recreate. Its virality is driven less by originality and more by its alignment with recognizable wellness aesthetics that perform well in platform algorithms.

Case Study 2: Non-Aesthetic Yogurt Bowl

Post: Payton, “Baked Banana Yogurt Bowl” (TikTok, Feb. 19) | Platform: TikTok | Date Analyzed: April 21, 2026

Engagement Metrics: 406 followers | 4,580 views | 153 likes | 4 comments | 64 saves | 8 shares

Analysis

Context & Background

This post presents a yogurt bowl in a much more casual and functional way. Unlike the first example, this bowl is framed less as a curated lifestyle object and more as a practical, high-protein breakfast idea. The creator emphasizes utility through macros and satiety rather than visual perfection, positioning the meal as efficient and realistic rather than aspirational.

Key Content Characteristics

The visual presentation is noticeably less polished. The bowl is photographed in a casual kitchen setting with visible stovetop burners in frame, less controlled lighting, and a less refined composition. Rather than careful ingredient placement, the bowl appears assembled for function, with toppings layered naturally and without strong visual symmetry.

The dominant visual focus is the baked banana topping, which appears warm, dense, and less visually "clean" than the fruit in the first post. The bowl is messier and more realistic, prioritizing texture and substance over visual polish. Text on screen emphasizes nutritional value directly: "360 cals | 23g protein | 6g fiber." This shifts attention away from aesthetics and toward measurable health benefits.

How It Supports My Research

This post supports my argument by showing the contrast between visually aesthetic health content and function-driven health content. While both meals may be nutritionally similar, this version is framed through practicality and nutritional utility rather than beauty. It demonstrates that health content does not need to be highly aesthetic to communicate value, but aesthetic presentation often shapes how appealing or aspirational that value appears.

Notable Aesthetic & Cultural Elements

This post reflects a more practical "realistic wellness" style often seen in macro-focused or fitness-adjacent content. Rather than aspirational softness, it emphasizes convenience, protein, and satiety. Its tone is less curated and more direct, aligning with a version of wellness culture centered on performance and efficiency rather than visual lifestyle branding.

Engagement Patterns

This post has noticeably lower engagement than the aesthetic example, suggesting that visually polished wellness content may perform better than equally nutritious but less curated alternatives. While still useful and informative, this post is less visually optimized for virality. Its value comes from practicality, but its lower aesthetic appeal likely reduces shareability and aspirational engagement.

Comparative Analysis

Similarities

Both posts present yogurt bowls as markers of health and wellness, using similar ingredients and promoting breakfast as a healthy lifestyle ritual. Both frame yogurt bowls as desirable, easy wellness choices and position food as part of a larger identity around self-care and health consciousness.

Differences

The main difference is how health is visually communicated. The aesthetic bowl frames health through beauty, order, and lifestyle aspiration. The non-aesthetic bowl frames health through nutrition, convenience, and performance. One sells health through visual identity, while the other sells it through measurable function.

Patterns

Together, these posts show that health content on social media is not just about what is eaten, but how it is visually packaged. Aesthetic presentation influences how health is perceived, making visually curated meals appear more desirable, elevated, and aspirational even when their nutritional value is similar to less polished alternatives.

Implications

These examples suggest that social media often teaches users to recognize health through aesthetic cues rather than nutritional substance. Visual presentation becomes a major factor in what content is perceived as healthy, aspirational, and worth engaging with.

Broader Trends & Patterns

  • Health is often communicated visually before it is communicated nutritionally
  • Clean, minimal, symmetrical food styling increases perceived wellness value
  • Soft lighting, neutral tones, and polished composition perform as markers of "healthy"
  • Functional health content (protein, macros, convenience) is often less visually rewarded
  • Social media favors wellness that is not only nutritious, but also aesthetically aspirational
  • The algorithm appears to reward health content that is visually branded, cohesive, and lifestyle-oriented more than content that is simply informative
