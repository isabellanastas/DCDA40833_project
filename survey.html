Survey Results

Data, Findings & Analysis

Survey Methodology

Provide details about how you conducted your survey:

  • Sample Size: 31 respondants
  • Demographics: Women, aged 18-23
  • Survey Period: April 21 through May 4
  • Distribution Method: Through text groups and social media
  • Question Format: Multiple choice, open-ended, and select all

Overview

The survey results showed a clear relationship between visual presentation and how people define “healthy.” Respondents consistently chose the more aesthetic option as both healthier and more appealing, even when the food itself was nearly identical. The findings also showed that social media plays a major role in shaping how users discover food and wellness content, reinforcing the idea that visual trends strongly influence perception.

Finding 1: Aesthetic Presentation Influences Perceived Health

Every respondent selected Option A as the healthier choice, showing a unanimous preference for the more visually curated version. This suggests that presentation alone can strongly shape perceptions of health, even before ingredients or nutrition are considered. The result highlights how aesthetics influence what people assume is “better for you.”

Finding 2: People Prefer What Looks Better

93.5% of respondents said they would rather eat Option A, the more aesthetic version of the meal. This shows that visual appeal does not just shape health perception, but also directly affects desirability. In other words, people are more likely to choose food that looks visually polished, even when the actual difference may be minimal.

Finding 3: “Healthy” Is Often Defined by Visual Cues

When asked why they chose their preferred option, 80.6% of respondents said it was because it looked better visually. Very few respondents selected reasons tied to actual nutrition or ingredients. This suggests that many people rely on visual cues like styling, color, and presentation to judge health, often using appearance as a shortcut for quality.

Finding 4: Social Media Strongly Shapes Food and Wellness Perception

100% of respondents said they have turned to social media for both food/drink and health/wellness recommendations. This reinforces how heavily users rely on platforms like TikTok and Instagram to guide what they eat and how they define wellness. Social media is not just influencing trends, but actively shaping everyday health decisions.

Key Survey Insights

Highlight the most important takeaways from your survey data:

  • Finding 1: Aesthetic presentation strongly shapes health perception, with 100% of respondents choosing the more visually polished option as healthier.
  • Finding 2: People are more likely to choose what looks better, as 93.5% said they would prefer to eat the more aesthetic option.
  • Finding 3: Visual appeal often defines what people consider healthy, with 80.6% saying they chose their preferred option simply because it looked better.
  • Finding 4: Social media plays a major role in shaping food and wellness decisions, with 100% of respondents saying they use it for recommendations.

Statistical Analysis

If applicable, provide statistical analysis of your data:

  • Correlation findings: Responses showed a strong relationship between aesthetic presentation and perceived health, suggesting that visual styling directly impacts judgment.
  • Significance testing results: While the sample size is small, response patterns were highly consistent, especially in the unanimous and near-unanimous results.
  • Demographic breakdowns: Most respondents fell within the college-aged demographic, making the findings especially relevant to Gen Z social media users.
  • Trend analysis: Across all responses, participants consistently associated polished visuals with healthier, more desirable food choices.

Survey Limitations

What are the limitations of your survey data?

  • Sample size or representativeness issues: The survey had a relatively small sample size (31 responses), which limits how broadly the findings can be generalized.
  • Response bias or self-selection bias: Respondents likely had existing familiarity with social media and wellness trends, which may have influenced answers.
  • Geographic or temporal limitations: Responses were collected within a limited timeframe and mostly reflect a similar age and social demographic.
  • Methodological constraints: Because responses were perception-based, the survey reflects subjective judgments rather than objective nutritional understanding.
